    The Internet and Advancements

    Computer Network Key Chronological Events.

    1. 1958- ARPA (Advanced Research Projects Agency) was created to improve technological advances.
    2. 1968- First people who worked in the network idea or project to have computers to communicate.
      1. Bob Taylor wanted to build one single network and asked for funding for work in a computer network.
      2. Lawrence Roberts created the first network between two computers and designed the computer network.
    3. 1969- All four sites were interconnected: UCLA, SRI, UCSB, University of Utah.
    4. 1971- ARPANET had 18 mainframe computers connected to the network.
    5. 1971- Email application (Killer app) introduced by Raymond Tomlinson.
    6. 1972- ARPANET demonstrated the computer network in Washington DC.
    8. 1973- Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn worked together to develop rules and procedures known as TCP/IP Protocol that would allow passing packets back and forth.
    9. 1983- TCP/IP was introduced as the universal standard of protocols.
    10. 1992- The Internet became public by a bill passed by Congress and became law a few months after.
